Bethany Hamilton: A Role Model for Young Girls

Why is She a Role Model?:

Bethany Hamilton used her faith in God and her faith in herself to get back in the water. Her determination inspires thousands all over the world.


Where is She now?

Now, in 2019, Bethany is raising her family with her husband.


Bethany is also traveling worldwide for surfing competitions. She also is doing interviews and talks to inspire young girls and boys


Lastly, Bethany is on an international tour promoting her new surf documentary, "Unstoppable".

Biography:

Bethany Meilani Hamilton was born in Lihue, Kauai, Hawaii on February 8, 1990. Her parents names are Sheri and Tom Hamilton. She is the youngest of three and has two older brothers, their names are Noah and Timonthy. She began surfing competively when she was 8 years old.


Bethany often surfed with long time best friend Alana Blanchard. They were homeschooled together, by Bethany's mom, so the two girls could have more time for surfing. Alana was with Bethany when the shark attacked her.


On October 31, 2003, when Bethany Hamilton was just 13 years old, she was attacked by a 16 foot tiger shark and resulted in the loss of her left arm. She was rushed to the hospital, and immediately had surgery. She thankfully recovered in just a few days. About a month after she was attacked, she was back in the water practicing her surfing. After she lost her arm, she went on, and still continues to succeed in national and international competitions.


Now, Bethany is married to Adam Dirks and has two boys. The oldest is Tobias Dirks and the youngest is Wesley Phillip Dirks.

Awards and Accomplishments:

  • Bethany Hamilton won the ESPY Award Comeback Athlete and was also given the Courage Teen Choice Award.

  • In 2004, Bethany won the MTV Teen Choice Award for 'Courage'

  • Also in 2004, Bethany won the 'Women of the Year' award from Surf Industry Manufacturing Association. She wrote her autobiography 'Soul Surfer', which became an instant success world-wide.

  • In 2005, she won the "Mildred Babe Dirddrikson-Zacharias Courage Award" from the United States Sports Academy.

  • When Bethany Hamilton was 15 years old, two years after she lost her arm, she placed first United States National Under-18 Surf Championship. She also went on to get 3rd place in the Roxy Pro in Austrailia, this is considered to be one of her best wins yet!

  • In 2014, at 24 years old, Bethany won 1st place at the Surf N Sea Pipeline Pro

  • When Bethany was just 9 years old, she got her first major sponsorship for Rip Curl and Tim Carroll Surfboards. A huge step in Bethany's career.

What Bethany Hamilton means to Me!

When her movie, Soul Surfer came out in 2011. I fell in love with everything about Bethany Hamilton and the message she spread of love and faith.


Bethany Hamilton became my idol, I watched her movie more times than I can count and can quote the entire film by heart. I have read her book at least 10 times.


I believe that Bethany Hamilton is such an inspiration because she wasn't afraid of the ocean, she wasn't afraid of being attacked again. She was afraid of not trying. That is why Bethany Hamilton is my role model.


When I was 11 years old, my mom found out that Bethany Hamilton was having a meet and greet in Ocean City, New Jersey. She told me and we left at 4:30 a.m and waited 9 hours for her to come. Once she came, I immediately got so happy and can say that it was on of the best days of my life.
